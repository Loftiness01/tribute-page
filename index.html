Pablo Picasso (1881-1973)


A Short Biography of Pablo Picasso:


  • Picasso was born on October 25, 1881 in the city of Malaga, Spain
  • His real name is Pablo Diego Jose Francisco de Paula John Nepomuceno Maria de Los Patricio Picasso
  • He received his name when he was baptized
  • His long name comes from a list of saint and relatives
  • His first name was "lapiz", the spanish word for pencil
  • His father was an artist, art professor and a museum curator
  • Pablo completed his first painting when he was nine years old. The title of his painting was Le Picador. The image is of man riding a horse in a bull fight
  • His father decided that he would give up painting when Picasso was thirteen years old because he felt his son was already a better painter than him
  • He studied at the Royal Academy of San Fernando
  • Picasso was extremely fond of the works of El Greco
  • In between his Blue and Rose period, Pablo Picasso moved to France
  • Pablo's first job was with an art dealer named Pere Menach
  • Picasso was a co-founder in the movement known as Cubism
  • The Nazis would not let Picasso display his painting in Germany
  • Picasso's artwork can be found in art museum throughout the world
  • Pablo had many lovers throughout his life. He often painted pictures of one of his lovers named Dharma
  • Picasso had four different children by three different women
  • He was married twice
  • Pablo passed away on April 08th, 1973 in Mougins, France
